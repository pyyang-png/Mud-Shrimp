                    <p><span class="highlight">Target Species:</span> Mud shrimp (<i>Austinogebia edulis</i>). An economically important seafood species protected within Marine Protected Areas (MPAs) in western Taiwan.</p>

                    <p><span class="highlight">The Issue:</span> Despite a decade of protection, there has been no significant improvement in their abundance.</p>

                    <p><span class="highlight">The Threat (PFBA):</span> As benthic organisms, mud shrimp accumulate Perfluorobutanoic acid (PFBA) through sediment and water. PFBA currently has the highest detection rate among eight PFAS studied in these coastal waters.</p>

                    <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-comments"></i> 4. Discussion</h2>

                    <ul>

                        <li><span class="highlight">Inverse Relationship:</span> The data exhibits a clear inverse relationship between PFBA concentrations and mud shrimp density.</li>

                        <li><span class="highlight">Site Comparison:</span> Wanggong MPA shows much higher PFBA levels in both sediment and biota compared to Shengang, corresponding with a significantly lower mud shrimp abundance (13 vs. 40 ind. m⁻²).</li>

                        <li><span class="highlight">Bioaccumulation:</span> The high accumulation of PFBA in benthic tissue at Wanggong (52.9 ng g⁻¹) confirms the active transfer of these "forever chemicals" from the sediment directly into the marine food web.</li>

                    </ul>

                </div>

 

                <div class="box conclusion-box flex-grow">

                    <h2><i class="fa-solid fa-lightbulb"></i> 5. Conclusion & Recommendations</h2>

                    <p><span class="highlight">Key Finding:</span> Sediment environments with lower concentrations of PFBA harbor healthier and significantly more abundant mud shrimp populations.</p>

                    <p style="margin-top: 15px; font-weight: 600;">Management Recommendations:</p>

                    <ol style="margin-left: 30px; margin-top: 10px;">

                        <li>Implement frequent environmental monitoring specifically for PFBA occurrences.</li>

                        <li>Conduct focused toxicological studies on the bioaccumulation features and physiological impacts of PFAS on marine benthos.</li>

                        <li>Address the transfer pathways of PFAS through the coastal food chain to formulate more effective long-term conservation strategies.</li>
